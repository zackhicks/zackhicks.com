AI Doesn't Have a Technology Problem. It Has a Leadership Problem.

July 2026

Most large companies have proven that AI works. They have pilots that improve productivity, intelligent agents that automate work, and prototypes that demonstrate real business value. Yet many of those same organizations struggle to move beyond isolated successes.

The obstacle isn't the technology. It's leadership.

The most difficult phase of an AI initiative begins after the prototype succeeds. That's when an organization has to integrate a new capability into the business, change operating processes, assign ownership, invest in scale, and often rethink how work gets done. At that point, AI is competing for attention with ERP programs, acquisitions, cybersecurity initiatives, cost reduction efforts, and every other priority already on the executive agenda.

Without visible sponsorship from a senior executive, AI initiatives often lose momentum. Not because the technology failed, but because the organization reverted to its existing priorities.

I believe companies should treat this stage differently. Emerging AI capabilities need executive sponsorship that extends beyond funding. They need a leader who is accountable for removing organizational barriers, aligning priorities across functions, and protecting the initiative until it becomes part of normal operations.

That protection is important because enterprise AI doesn't fit neatly into traditional governance. It cuts across business units, technology, data, legal, finance, HR, and risk. Without someone empowered to make decisions across those boundaries, progress slows and enthusiasm fades.

I've seen organizations celebrate successful pilots that never reached production. I've also seen organizations create lasting competitive advantage because a senior executive made scaling AI a business priority rather than a technology project.

For boards, this changes the conversation. The question is no longer, “How many AI pilots do we have?” The better question is, “Who is personally accountable for taking our most promising AI capabilities from prototype to enterprise scale?”

Technology creates the opportunity. Leadership determines whether the opportunity becomes enterprise value.

Question for the Boardroom
Who in your organization owns the successful transition from AI prototype to enterprise capability?
